Types Of Disasters In The Bay Area

Data Collected From 1980-2010

Wildfires

Wildfires are very common in the Bay Area. They have wiped out over 7.6 million acres in California over the past 10 years. These fires can start from dry vegetation, human-caused ignition, and intense heat. With higher temperatures, the Bay Area is more prone to wildfires and other disasters.

Earthquakes

Earthquakes occur frequently in the bay area, and San Francisco Tourism Tips reports that around 5,000 or more earthquakes occur “in and around the San Francisco Bay Area” each year. The largest earthquake in San Francisco's history was the 1906 San Francisco earthquake, which reached 7.9 on the Richter Scale.

Floods

Floods, often caused by heavy amounts of rainfall or river flooding, are a natural disaster that is common in the bay area. These disasters can happen at anytime and cause widespread damage and loss of life.
